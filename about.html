About Us

EMPOWERING COLLEGIATE WOMEN INTERESTED IN BUSINESS

The Intercollegiate Business Convention (IBC) is the largest undergraduate conference in the world. It is also the keystone event organized by Harvard Undergraduate Women in Business (HUWIB). IBC 2014 will be held on Saturday, October 18 at the Hynes Convention Center in Boston.

This year, the objective of IBC is to build a global support network and community of empowerment for collegiate women interested in business.

Now in its tenth year, IBC attracts over 1,200 registrants and over 100 professionals from a diverse range of industries. Attendees will learn from three inspiring keynote speakers, interact with peers and professionals, and attend an exclusive career fair and networking session.

KEYNOTE SPEAKERS

Lyndsey Scott

MODEL, ACTRESS, APP DEVELOPER

Three years after graduating from Amherst college in 2006 with dual majors in computer science and theater, Lyndsey found herself catapulted into the international world of high-fashion after becoming the first African-American model to be awarded an exclusice contract with Calvin Klein. Since then, she has walked the runways for clients such as Prada, Gucci, Fendi, Louis Vuitton, and Victoria's Secret and appeared in campaigns for clients such as DKNY and CoverGirl.

Although computer science has never completely left her life since her first coding experiences on a TI-89 as a young teen, over the past few years, Lyndsey has devoted much of her energy to programming iOS apps, several of which are currently available in the Apple app store. Lyndsey also devotes her time to promoting computer science education to young people and especially makes an effort to get more women and minorities into the field.

Jill Abramson

AUTHOR, EDITOR, JOURNALIST

Jill Abramson is a journalist who spent the last 17 years in the most senior editorial positions at The New York Times, where she was the first woman to serve as Washington Bureau Chief, Managing Editor and Executive Editor. Before joining The Times, she spent nine years at The Wall Street Journal as the deputy Washington Bureau Chief and an investigative reporter covering money and politics.

She is the author of three books including Strange Justice, which she co-authored with Jane Mayer. In addition to her current position as a lecturer in Harvard's English department, Ms. Abramson has taught at both Princeton and Yale, where she led undergraduate writing seminars for five years. She is a member of the American Academy of Arts & Sciences and the American Philosophical Society.

Maureen Chiquet

GLOBAL CEO, CHANEL

As global Chief Executive Officer of Chanel, Maureen Chiquet oversees and coordinates activities worldwide in addition to developing the organization's active and conscious leadership initiative. Joining the company in 2003, Ms. Chiquet was named president of Chanel, Inc., in the United States in 2004, and became the company's first global CEO in 2007.

Ms. Chiquet began her career at L'Oreal Paris in 1985. In 1988 she joined Gap, and in 1994 she was recruited to help launch Old Navy. Over eight years, she advanced to Executive Vice President, growing the brand from 35 to 850 stores, before becoming President of Banana Republic in 2002.

She has been recognized among Fortune's "International Power 50", Forbes' "100 Most Powerful Women", The Wall Street Journal's "50 Women to Watch" and Glamour Magazine's "Women of the Year". She currently sits on the Board of Directors of children's clothing company Peek... Aren't You Curious and the Board of Trustees of the New York Academy of Art.

IBC Talks are shorter, TED-style presentations that will feature emerging industry leaders. These talks will showcase women of a diverse range of careers and backgrounds who inspire and positively impact the changing global business landscape.
